Project Requirements

Detailed analysis and documentation of the Portalt project requirements

Partner Introduction

IBM is a global technology company with a rich history of innovation spanning over a century. Their Innovation Centre at York Road, Waterloo serves as a hub for showcasing cutting-edge technologies and solutions to clients and partners. The centre demonstrates IBM's latest advancements in areas such as quantum computing, artificial intelligence, cloud solutions, and more.

The Innovation Centre plays a crucial role in IBM's business strategy, allowing potential clients to experience firsthand how IBM's technologies can transform their operations. However, the physical limitations of the centre mean that international clients and partners often face challenges in accessing these demonstrations without traveling to the UK.

Project Background

Currently, IBM faces a significant challenge with their Innovation Centre: international clients cannot experience the centre's offerings without physically traveling to the UK. This limitation restricts IBM's ability to showcase their innovations to a global audience efficiently.

For example, a potential client in the UAE interested in IBM's quantum computing solutions would need to arrange international travel to visit the Innovation Centre in Waterloo. This creates barriers in terms of time, cost, and logistics, potentially impacting business opportunities.

Our project addresses this challenge by creating an augmented reality (AR) application that enables remote visitors to virtually experience the Innovation Centre. This solution will leverage the UBIQ online environment to create an immersive, interactive experience that captures the essence of being physically present at the centre.

The application will allow multiple participants from different locations to simultaneously visit the virtual Innovation Centre, interact with 3D models of IBM's products (such as their quantum computer), and engage with both IBM representatives and an AI-powered chatbot for information.

Additionally, the solution is designed to be adaptable for other contexts, such as NHS environments where medical students could remotely learn about specific organs or procedures with guidance from professors.

Project Goals

Our primary aim is to develop an innovative augmented reality solution that breaks down geographical barriers for IBM's Innovation Centre, enabling global access to their cutting-edge technologies while creating a template that can be adapted for various industries. The project will focus on creating an immersive, interactive experience that closely mimics physical presence while leveraging AI to enhance user interactions.

Immersive Multiplayer Environment

Create an immersive multiplayer AR environment that allows IBM representatives and international clients to interact in real-time as VR avatars, providing remote access to the Innovation Centre's offerings.

Intuitive Admin Interface

Develop an intuitive admin interface for IBM staff to easily upload, position, and manage 3D models of their products in the virtual environment, ensuring content remains consistent for all users.

AI-Powered Interactions

Implement AI-powered interactions through a RAG-enabled chatbot with voice responses and Granite as an offline AI processor, enhancing the information delivery experience.

3D Model Research

Research and integrate 3D model generative AI to identify optimal methods for adding new content to the virtual environment efficiently.

Scalable Open-Source Solution

Design a scalable, open-source solution that serves as a template for other use cases beyond IBM, such as NHS training environments, with comprehensive documentation to support wider adoption.

Requirement Gathering

User Interviews and Personas

As part of our requirements gathering process, we conducted a series of semi-structured interviews and created detailed user personas to better understand user needs and expectations for the AR experience.

End User

VR/AR Experience Visitor

Goals

Experience virtual tours with engaging, interactive content. Easily navigate and access information.

Challenges

May have varying levels of technical expertise and comfort with VR/AR technology.

Preferences

Prioritizes intuitive navigation, clear information presentation, and immersive experiences.

Persona Sketch
Interview Responses
How familiar are you with AR or VR technology? Have you used anything similar before?

"Not really. I've seen videos of people using VR for gaming, but I've never tried it myself. I'm curious, though! It looks cool, and I'd like to see how it can help me understand complex topics."

During the workshop, would you prefer more guidance or a chance to explore on your own with some direction?

"A bit of both, I think. I like to start with some guidance, so I know what I'm looking at. But after that, it would be fun to explore on my own. I think I'd learn more if I could go at my own pace and check things out without too much pressure."

What aspects of AR/VR technology are you most excited about or interested in exploring?

"A bit of both, I think. I like to start with some guidance, so I know what I'm looking at. But after that, it would be fun to explore on my own. I think I'd learn more if I could go at my own pace and check things out without too much pressure."

Lastly, what would make this experience memorable and enjoyable for you?

"For me, it's about being able to understand something really complex in a simple way. If I can come away feeling like I 'get' Quantum Computing, even just a little, I'd be happy. Also, if there's a way to share what I've learned, like a summary I can take away, that would be great."

Admin User

Tech-Savvy Professional

Goals

Create and customize virtual tours for their organization or clients. Manage content and user access.

Challenges

Needs an intuitive interface to efficiently create professional-looking tours without extensive technical knowledge.

Preferences

Values customization options, ease of use, and the ability to update content regularly.

Persona Sketch
Interview Responses
To start, could you describe the current setup at the Innovation Centre? What kinds of displays or technology are used to engage visitors, and how effective do you feel they are?

"Right now, we have several large screens with videos and presentations showcasing IBM's history and innovations. We also have some basic interactive kiosks for visitors to explore different projects. While these are helpful, I think they're a bit limited in immersiveness—visitors can only learn so much from passive displays, and I feel like we could engage them on a deeper level with something more interactive."

What kind of customization would you like to see for the specific content shown in each experience? For example, would you want control over which 3D models, infographics, or key landmarks are featured?

"Yes, definitely. It would be great to have a library of content, like 3D models and infographics, where I could pick and choose what's relevant for each tour. If I could arrange these into a sequence, like a timeline or a specific path, that would make it easy to tell a story or highlight key points depending on the group."

How important is branding customization for you? Would you want options to adjust IBM branding or incorporate specific visuals to match different themes?

"It's important—since we might have visiting partners or special events where we need to tailor the branding a bit. If could adjust logos, colours, or even add certain slides or images relevant to specific clients, that would make the experience feel much more professional and personalized."

From your perspective, how would you use this AR software as part of your role? Do you imagine it would be helpful for guided tours, special events, or even specific visitor groups?

"Definitely. For guided tours, it would make the experience much richer if I could activate different scenarios based on the audience. For instance, when hosting a group of students, I could highlight IBM's contributions to education and present the information in a fun, interactive way. For client-focused events, I could tailor it to show how IBM tech fits into different industries. If the app could easily save and load different themes, that would make presentations more impactful."

Key Conclusions from User Research

Based on our interviews and persona development, we identified these critical insights about what users value most:

  1. Intuitive Navigation with Flexible Guidance
    Users prioritize an interface that's easy to navigate with optional guidance. They prefer starting with basic orientation then exploring independently at their own pace.
  2. Content Customization and Flexibility
    Administrators need straightforward tools to customize content for different audiences, with the ability to select relevant models and information based on specific visitor groups or events.
  3. Complex Information Made Accessible
    Users value experiences that simplify complex technical concepts through visualization and interaction, with takeaway summaries for later reference.
  4. Professional Presentation with Adaptable Branding
    The solution must support professional-level presentation with adaptable branding options that can be tailored to different clients, partners, or special events.

Admin User Use Cases

ID Use Case Description

MoSCoW Requirements

Functional Requirements

Priority ID Requirement
Must Have F1 The system must provide an intuitive interface for administrators to create and manage VR scenes.
F2 The system must allow administrators to select from preset environments or import custom environments to recreate physical spaces.
F3 The system must enable viewing experiences on both VR headsets and PC platforms.
F4 The system must support real-time communication between users in the same virtual environment.
F5 The system must include an AI chatbot that can provide voice responses to user queries.
F6 The system must allow navigation within the VR environment using VR controllers or keyboard inputs (when using PC).
F7 The system must include a library of preset 3D objects for administrators to select from.
Should Have F8 The system should support multiplayer functionality allowing multiple users to interact within the same virtual space.
F9 The system should allow administrators to upload and use custom 3D objects.
F10 The system should dynamically load 3D objects into the executable without requiring code changes.
F11 The system should provide analytics on user engagement and interaction patterns.
F12 The system should enable customization of AI chatbot parameters and reference materials.
Could Have F13 The system could provide voice command functionality for navigating the environment.
F14 The system could include interactive elements like quizzes or guided tours.
F15 The system could allow for real-time editing of environments while users are present.
F16 The system could provide templates for different types of virtual spaces (classroom, exhibition hall, etc.).
Won't Have F17 The system won't have built-in 3D modeling capabilities.
F18 The system won't support direct integration with enterprise management systems in the initial release.
F19 The system won't have AR/VR authoring capabilities within the VR environment itself.

Non-Functional Requirements

Priority ID Category Requirement
Must Have NF1 Usability The admin interface must be intuitive enough to be used with minimal training.
NF2 Performance The VR environment must maintain a minimum of 72 FPS on supported VR headsets to prevent motion sickness.
NF3 Compatibility The admin dashboard must be usable on Windows, macOS, and Linux operating systems.
NF4 Compatibility The viewer application must be compatible with major VR headsets (Meta Quest, HTC Vive) and standard PCs.
NF5 Reliability The system must operate without crashes for at least 2 hours of continuous use.
NF6 Security The system must implement user authentication for administrative access.
NF7 Portability The system must allow scene creation without requiring users to modify source code.
Should Have NF8 Scalability The system should support at least 10 concurrent users in a single multiplayer room without performance degradation.
NF9 Performance The system should load 3D objects within 3 seconds when entering a new environment.
NF10 Usability The system should provide visual feedback for all user interactions within 200ms.
NF11 Accessibility The system should include subtitles for AI voice responses and high-contrast visual options.
NF12 Maintainability The system should use a modular architecture to facilitate future updates and extensions.
Could Have NF13 Localization The system could support multiple languages for both UI and AI interactions.
NF14 Performance The system could optimize memory usage to support environments with 50+ detailed 3D objects.
NF15 Scalability The system could support scaling to 30+ concurrent users in a single virtual space.
NF16 Interoperability The system could provide an API for third-party integrations and extensions.
Won't Have NF17 Compatibility The system won't support obsolete VR hardware (pre-2018 models) or mobile VR platforms in the initial release.
NF18 Performance The system won't support photorealistic rendering with real-time ray tracing in the initial release.
NF19 Security The system won't implement enterprise-grade security features like SSO or compliance with financial industry regulations.
