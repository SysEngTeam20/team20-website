Legal Issues and Processes

GDPR Compliance

Overview

Portalt is committed to protecting user data and privacy in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). This document outlines our approach to GDPR compliance and our data protection measures.

Data Collection Principles
  • Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency: We collect and process data with a clear legal basis and in a transparent manner.
  • Purpose limitation: Data is collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes only.
  • Data minimization: We limit data collection to what is necessary for the platform's functionality.
  • Accuracy: Measures are in place to ensure data accuracy and provide means for updates.
  • Storage limitation: Data is stored only for the duration necessary to fulfill its purpose.
  • Security: Appropriate technical and organizational measures protect against unauthorized processing and accidental loss.
User Rights

In accordance with GDPR, Portalt users have the right to:

  • Access their personal data
  • Rectify inaccurate data
  • Erase their data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Restrict processing of their data
  • Data portability (receive their data in a structured, commonly used format)
  • Object to data processing
  • Not be subject to automated decision-making, including profiling
Implementation Measures
  • Data Protection Officer: A designated team member oversees GDPR compliance.
  • Data Protection Impact Assessments: Conducted for high-risk processing activities.
  • Privacy by Design: Data protection incorporated into system architecture.
  • Breach Notification: Procedures in place to report data breaches within 72 hours.
  • Consent Management: Clear, granular consent options with easy withdrawal mechanism.
  • Third-party Processors: Data processing agreements with all service providers.
Contact Information

For GDPR-related inquiries or to exercise your data rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer at dpo@portalt.com.

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 15, 2025

This Privacy Policy describes how Portalt ("we", "our", or "us") collects, uses, and shares your personal information when you use our virtual tour platform.

Information We Collect

Personal Information

  • Account Information: Name, email address, password, and profile information when you create an account.
  • User Content: Virtual tours, annotations, customizations, and other content you create, upload, or store on the platform.
  • Communications: Information you provide when contacting our support team or participating in surveys and feedback sessions.

Automatically Collected Information

  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, browser type, IP address, and unique device identifiers.
How We Use Your Information
  • Providing and improving our platform functionality
  • Processing transactions and managing your account
  • Personalizing your experience and content
  • Communicating with you about updates, features, and support
  • Analyzing usage patterns to enhance platform performance
  • Ensuring security and preventing fraud
  • Complying with legal obligations
Information Sharing

We may share your information with:

  • Business Partners: With your consent, for integrations or joint offerings
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, legal process, or to protect our rights
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information, including encryption, access controls, and regular security assessments. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to provide our services, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. When no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymize your information.

Your Choices
  • Account Information: Update or correct your information through your account settings
  • Cookies: Manage cookie preferences through your browser settings
  • Data Access and Deletion: Request a copy of your data or account deletion through our Data Protection Officer
Children's Privacy

Portalt is not intended for children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or for legal, operational, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any material changes through the platform or by email.

Contact Us

If you have questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact our Data Protection Officer at privacy@portalt.com.

Data Governance

Data Governance Framework

Portalt implements a structured data governance framework to ensure responsible management of all data throughout its lifecycle. This framework establishes policies, procedures, and standards for data quality, security, privacy, and compliance.

Data Classification

We classify data into the following categories to determine appropriate handling procedures:

  • Public Data: Information that can be freely shared (e.g., public virtual tour content, marketing materials)
  • Internal Data: Non-sensitive information for internal use (e.g., non-personal analytics, operational metrics)
  • Confidential Data: Sensitive information requiring protection (e.g., user credentials, payment information)
  • Restricted Data: Highly sensitive information with strict access controls (e.g., private virtual tour content, personal identifiable information)
Data Ownership and Stewardship
  • Data Owners: Department heads responsible for specific data domains
  • Data Stewards: Individuals who manage data quality, metadata, and access controls
  • Data Custodians: IT staff responsible for technical management of data storage and security
  • Data Users: All personnel who access and use data in their roles
Data Quality Management

To maintain high data quality standards, Portalt implements:

  • Data Quality Metrics: Regular assessment of accuracy, completeness, consistency, and timeliness
  • Data Validation Rules: Automated checks during data entry and processing
  • Data Cleansing Procedures: Regular audits and corrections of data anomalies
  • Master Data Management: Centralized control of key data entities
Data Lifecycle Management

We manage data through its entire lifecycle:

  1. Creation/Acquisition: Data collection with proper consent and validation
  2. Storage: Secure database systems with appropriate access controls
  3. Usage: Monitored data access and processing aligned with stated purposes
  4. Archiving: Transferring inactive data to long-term storage when appropriate
  5. Deletion: Secure data removal when retention periods expire or upon request
Data Access Controls

Access to data is managed through:

  • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Permissions assigned based on job responsibilities
  • Principle of Least Privilege: Users granted minimum access needed for their role
  • Authentication Requirements: Multi-factor authentication for accessing sensitive data
  • Access Review Process: Regular audits of access permissions
Data Retention Policy

Portalt retains different types of data according to the following guidelines:

  • User Account Data: Retained while account is active; deleted or anonymized within 30 days of account closure
  • Virtual Tour Content: Retained as long as specified by content creator; default retention period of 3 years for inactive content
  • Usage Analytics: Retained in identifiable form for 12 months; thereafter anonymized for long-term analysis
  • Communication Records: Retained for 2 years to support customer service and legal requirements
Data Incident Response

In the event of a data breach or security incident:

  1. Detection and Assessment: Immediate evaluation of incident scope and impact
  2. Containment: Swift action to limit data exposure and prevent further breach
  3. Notification: Communication with affected users and relevant authorities within required timeframes
  4. Recovery: Restoration of systems and data integrity
  5. Post-Incident Analysis: Investigation of root causes and implementation of preventive measures
Data Governance Committee

Our Data Governance Committee meets quarterly to review policies, address data quality issues, ensure compliance with regulations, and adapt our framework to evolving needs. The committee includes representatives from IT, Legal, Operations, and User Experience departments.

Training and Awareness

All team members receive regular training on data governance policies, privacy regulations, and security best practices. We foster a culture of data responsibility throughout the organization.
